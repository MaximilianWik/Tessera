𓌹 EC PRIMER 𓌺

Reed-Solomon redundancy. Extra codewords are appended to the data so a partially damaged QR still decodes back to the exact input. Four standard levels:

L
~7%clean digital displays
M
~15%paper print, controlled
Q
~25%outdoor wear, tattoos
H
~30%heavy abuse, logo cutouts

Percentages are codeword-level, not visual area. Concentrated damage usually exhausts a single block's correction budget before reaching the headline number.

    IV.   Damage preview · blur (tattoo aging)

    ·

    Real tattoos fail through gradual ink bleed. This applies a Gaussian blur to the QR at the chosen severity and re-decodes. The tolerance log on the right runs every level on load and tells you the highest blur the QR survives unaided.

    Damage 0%

    5% ≈ a fresh tattoo after a few years. 15% ≈ a 15-year-old tattoo with normal aging. 30% is heavily blurred.
